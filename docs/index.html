our mission

SkyPrecision is a project designed around the precision viticulture needs of Blenheim Vineyard. Our mission is to streamline the crop monitoring process for our users so that they can amplify crop output while also reducing time spent in the field. To accomplish that goal, the project uses drone-based multispectral imagery to inform an interactive web-based platform on vine health.

SkyPrecision provides two primary competencies to the user. First, it gives them an advanced view of crop health, allowing them to see where and how their crops may be at risk. Pulling multispectral images from drone coverage, it allows the user to identify areas of concern based on water content, thermal imagery, and established vegetation indices. Second, it gives the user the capability to interact with the crops, providing the functionality to manually diagnose and geo-tag specific vines directly from the field.

Currently, the application is under development, but the team will be completing regular milestones over the course of the next 6 months in order to fully meet the needs of our user.

why skyprecision?

SkyPrecision offers a convenient toolkit for high-tech agriculture using aerial drones and precise multispectral and thermal imagery, as well as photogrammetry. Users can view vegetation indices, digital surface models, thermal imagery, and make geo-referenced notes. Interact with the map below to learn more!

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI)

The NDVI is often used to assess general crop health for the purpose of fertilizer application. A value of -1 means very unhealthy crops, while a value of 1 indicates very healthy growth.

Normalized Difference Red Edge (NDRE)

The NDRE can be used to estimate leaf chlorophyll content. This index is more applicable for late-season crops than the NDVI. This index is especially useful in estimating nitrogen content. Values between -1 and 0 typically represent soil and values between 0 and 1 represent vegetation, with healthier vegetation resulting in a higher value.

Infrared Imagery

The infrared camera can be used to determine the temperature of each location in a field. This can be a useful source of information for irrigation purposes.

Geo-Referenced Notes

SkyPrecision gives users the ability to save geo-referenced notes to mark areas of interest.
